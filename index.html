Frequently Asked Questions
   
      What is a notary public?      

A notary public is a public officer appointed by the state government to witness the signing of important documents and administer oaths and affirmations. The primary purpose of a notary is to deter fraud and ensure the authenticity of signatures.

   
   
      What do I need to bring for my notarization?      

You will need to bring the document(s) to be notarized and a valid, government-issued photo identification. Acceptable IDs include a driver's license, state ID card, or passport. The ID must be current and include a photo and signature.

   
   
      Can you notarize a document that is already signed?      

No, generally not. For most notarizations (like acknowledgments), the signer must appear in person before the notary and sign the document in the notary's presence, or acknowledge their signature at the time of notarization. For oaths/affirmations, the oath must be administered directly by the notary.

We primarily serve the greater Houston area. Please contact us with your specific location, and we will confirm our availability and any applicable trip fees.

   
   
      What payment methods do you accept?      

We accept various payment methods, including cash, Venmo, Zelle, Cash App, and all major credit/debit cards (Visa, MasterCard, American Express, Discover).

   
   
      Can you provide legal advice?      

As notaries public, we are prohibited from providing legal advice or explaining the contents of any document. Our role is strictly limited to witnessing signatures and administering oaths/affirmations.
