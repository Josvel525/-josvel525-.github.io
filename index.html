Frequently Asked Questions
Can a notary public give me legal advice? I AM NOT AN ATTORNEY LICENSED TO PRACTICE LAW IN TEXAS AND MAY NOT GIVE LEGAL ADVICE OR ACCEPT FEES FOR LEGAL ADVICE.
Do you offer after hours services? Absolutely, please text us at 832-356-0671 or email us at info@texaspublicnotary.us for after hours or other special requests!
Can notary publics in Texas perform notary acts in all counties in Texas? Yes, all active Texas commissioned notary publics can perform notary acts anywhere in the state of Texas.
Will I need a government issued identification card to obtain notarization services? Yes. You will need valid identification and the name on the identification card must match the name and signature of the document being notarized.
Can you notarize a document that is already signed? Yes, but all individuals who’s name and signature are to be notarized must be there in person, with valid identification, at the time your document is notarized.
What are other requirements to obtain notarizations?
  1. The signer has been positively identified by the Texas notary.
  2. The signer has personally appeared before the Texas notary to sign the document willingly, without any coercion, and for the purpose stated on the document.
  3. The Texas notary performed the notarial act on the date stated on the notarial certificate.
