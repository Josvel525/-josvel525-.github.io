Frequently Asked Questions
What do I need for my document to be notarized?
  1. All signers must be present.
  2. All signers must have a valid, unexpired government-issued photo identification. Acceptable forms of ID include:
    • Texas Driver's License or ID Card
    • Out-of-state Driver's License or ID Card (if valid in that state)
    • U.S. Passport
    • Foreign Passport (must be stamped by U.S. Customs and Immigration Services)
    • U.S. Military ID
    • Mexican or Canadian Driver's License
  3. The document must be complete and ready for signature. Notaries cannot provide legal advice or prepare documents.
  4. If the document requires witnesses, the witnesses must be present at the time of notarization and also have valid identification. Notaries cannot act as witnesses.
What is a mobile notary? A mobile notary is a notary public who travels to a client's location (home, office, hospital, etc.) to perform notarization services. This offers convenience and flexibility, especially for individuals who cannot easily travel or need documents notarized outside of standard business hours.
What areas do you serve? We primarily serve the Greater Houston area, including but not limited to Houston, Spring, Cypress, Katy, Sugar Land, and surrounding communities. Please contact us to confirm availability in your specific location.
What are your hours of operation? We offer 24/7 mobile notary services by appointment. While we strive to accommodate all requests, after-hours fees may apply for services performed before 8 AM or after 7 PM, and on holidays.
Can you notarize a document that is in a foreign language? As a Texas Notary Public, I can notarize a document written in a foreign language as long as I can identify the type of notarial act required (e.g., acknowledgment, oath/affirmation). However, I cannot translate the document or certify its contents. The signer must understand what they are signing.
