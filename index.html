Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

   
      What is a mobile notary?      
           
  1. A mobile notary is a notary public who travels to a client's preferred location to perform notarizations. This offers convenience and flexibility, as clients don't need to travel to a notary's office.
    2.      
   
   
      What do I need for a notarization?      
           
  1. Valid, unexpired government-issued photo identification (e.g., driver's license, state ID, passport).
    2.        
  2. The document(s) to be notarized, complete and ready for signature. Do not sign the document beforehand if it requires a notarized signature.
    3.        
  3. All parties whose signatures require notarization must be present.
    4.      
   
   
      What forms of ID are acceptable?      
           
  1. State-issued Driver's License or ID card
    2.        
  2. U.S. Passport
    3.        
  3. Foreign Passport (stamped by USCIS)
    4.        
  4. U.S. Military ID
    5.        
  5. State-issued Photo ID Card
    6.        
  6. Driver's License issued by Canada or Mexico
    7.        
  7. Alien Registration Card / Green Card
    8.        
  8. Consular ID (some restrictions apply)
    9.      
   
   
      Can you notarize a document in a language other than English?      
           
  1. Yes, as long as the notary can communicate directly with the signer(s) in a language they both understand. The notary cannot translate the document or provide legal advice related to its content. The notarial certificate must be in English.
    2.      
   
   
      Do you offer Apostille services?      
           
  1. While we do not issue Apostilles ourselves, we can notarize your documents and assist with the preparation and submission process to the Texas Secretary of State's office for Apostille or Authentication. We can guide you through the requirements to ensure your documents are properly prepared.
    2.      
   
   
      What payment methods do you accept?      
           
  1. We accept cash, credit/debit cards (Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover), Zelle, and Venmo. Payment is due at the time of service.
    2.      
   
   
      What areas do you serve?      
           
  1. We proudly serve Houston, TX, and surrounding areas within a 50-mile radius. Please refer to our Trip Fees section for detailed pricing based on distance.
    2.      
   
   
      Can you provide legal advice or prepare documents?      
           
  1. As Notary Publics, we are prohibited by law from giving legal advice or drafting legal documents. Our role is to verify identities, witness signatures, and administer oaths or affirmations. For legal advice or document preparation, please consult with a qualified attorney.
    2.
