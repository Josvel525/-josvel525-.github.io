Can a Texas notary help with documents used in other states?
âœ… Yes. While a Texas notary public is only authorized to perform notarizations within the state of Texas, a document properly notarized under Texas law is generally recognized in all 50 states.

Once a notarial act is completed in accordance with Texas law, the document carries full legal weight across the country. This is due to the Full Faith and Credit Clause of the U.S. Constitution, which ensures that official acts, like notarizations, are honored by other states.

Unless a state has a specific legal objection to the content of a document (which is rare), notarized documents from Texas are accepted nationwide by courts, banks, government agencies, and businesses.
Can you perform notary acts in all Texas counties?
Yes, all active Texas commissioned notary publics can perform notary acts anywhere in the state of Texas.
Will I need a government issued ID?
Yes. You will need a valid, unexpired government-issued photo identification. The name on the ID must reasonably match the name on the document.
What are the requirements for notarization?
  1. The signer must be positively identified by the Notary.
  2. The signer must personally and willingly appear before the Notary.
  3. The Notary must perform the act on the date stated on the certificate.
